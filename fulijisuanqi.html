What is Compound Interest?

Compound interest refers to calculating interest not only on the principal but also on previously accumulated interest. Simply put, it's 'interest on interest'.

Unlike simple interest, which only calculates interest on the principal, compound interest allows your investment to grow exponentially.

Compound interest is the eighth wonder of the world. He who understands it, earns it; he who doesn't, pays it.

- Albert Einstein

Compound Interest Formula

The formula for compound interest is: A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

Where:

  • A = Future Value
  • P = Principal
  • r = Annual Interest Rate
  • n = Number of times interest is compounded per year
  • t = Number of years

Why is Compound Interest the Eighth Wonder?

Compound interest is called the eighth wonder because it has the amazing power to change the fate of wealth. Through time accumulation, even small investments can grow into huge wealth through the compound interest effect.

Application of Compound Interest in Investment

Understanding the compound interest effect is crucial for developing effective investment strategies. Here are the key factors for using compound interest to grow wealth:

1. Time - The Best Friend of Compound Interest

The longer the time, the more obvious the compound interest effect. Starting to invest early gives your money more time to grow.

2. Importance of Return Rate

Even small differences in return rates can produce huge differences in results under long-term compound interest.

3. The Power of Regular Investment

Regular fixed-amount investments can use 'dollar-cost averaging' to reduce risk while accelerating compound growth.

4. Compound Interest and the Rule of 72

The Rule of 72 is a simple way to estimate the time required for an investment to double: divide 72 by the annual return rate. For example, with an 8% annual return, the investment will double in about 9 years (72 Ã· 8 = 9).
